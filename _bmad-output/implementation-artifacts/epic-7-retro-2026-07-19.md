## What went well

1. **Accessibility baseline actually closed the gap (Kyle's highlight).** Story 7.3 didn't just patch a few ARIA labels — it closed the WCAG Level A gate for login, picks, and standings with a full manual keyboard/VoiceOver/contrast checklist (`docs/accessibility-checklist.md`), a theme-wide focus-visible ring, nav landmarks, and enriched `MatchupCard` aria-labels. Zero known Level A exceptions remained at story close.

2. **"What this story is (and is NOT)" discipline held all epic.** Every one of the 4 stories opened Dev Notes with an explicit in-scope/out-of-scope split and a "Reuse — do NOT reinvent" table pointing at the immediately preceding story. Scope stayed tight across a naturally sprawling epic (export, logging, a11y, perf all touch the same admin pages and shell).

3. **Adversarial review caught a real bug before production.** Story 7.4's dedicated multi-lens code review (2026-07-19, commit `426e2a2`) found that the email circuit breaker was scoped per-league instead of per-cron-invocation — meaning a full Resend outage would silently re-arm on every league instead of aborting the run. Fixed before merge.

4. **Deferred-work hygiene reached a new high-water mark.** `deferred-work.md` now shows ~18 items struck through with "Resolved by 7.X" annotations this epic alone (weather caching, skeleton loaders, 48px buttons, cron `maxDuration`, non-200 on failure, circuit breaker, redundant layout Prisma fetches). Almost nothing from this epic's own review sits unresolved without an owner or explicit accepted-risk note.

5. **Honest measurement over fake numbers.** Rather than fabricate Lighthouse/NFR5 numbers for picks/standings/pick-submit, the team documented **why** those specific measurements weren't captured (auth cookie handling, pre-season dev-seed state) and got explicit sign-off from Kyle to accept them as Known Exceptions rather than block the story.

6. **Reusable patterns compounding.** `getLeagueAccess` (`React.cache`) killed a redundant Prisma fetch between league layout and every child page in one shared loader — a pattern worth reaching for anywhere else double-fetching shows up. `mapWithConcurrency` + circuit breaker gives email sends both throughput and failure containment in one small module.

## Challenges and friction

1. **Review patch volume didn't visibly drop.** The Epic 6 retro's "email/API route checklist" action item didn't obviously reduce findings-per-story (7.1: 9, 7.2: 4, 7.3: 8, 7.4: 15). Read charitably, this tracks epic scope growing (a11y + perf + cron hardening cover far more surface area than a single feature), not sloppier execution — but it's worth watching in Epic 8.

2. **Two performance measurements remain incomplete.** `docs/performance-budgets.md` has real Lighthouse numbers only for `/login` (LCP 2.34s, TTI 3.36s); picks/standings need an authenticated re-measure. The pick-submit NFR5 sample was skipped because every dev-seed league is pre-season. Both accepted as Known Exceptions during 7.4's code review — **carried forward to `deferred-work.md`** at this retro rather than left buried in the story file (see Action Items).

3. **Circuit breaker / `EMAIL_CIRCUIT_OPEN` path is untested end-to-end.** The breaker logic has unit tests, but nothing has exercised it against a real simulated outage across a full cron invocation. Flagged by Dana as a natural fit for Epic 8.5 (email/cron in rehearsal).
